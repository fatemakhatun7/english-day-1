A. Sentence making

We need an action (for example: sing). This action is called the Verb. And the doer (for example, jesica). This doer is called Subject.

Example: Jesica sings.

An intransitive verb is a â€œverb that indicates a complete action without being accompanied by a direct object (the verb without the object).

Examples:

1. He runs.

2. She swims.

Intransitive verb accept prepositional phrase, adverb and cognate object (when the intransitive verb and the object are the same word, this is called cognate object).

Prepositional phrase:

1. The baby is sleeping in our room.

2. I can't believe our dog ran away from home..

3. I sneezed from the dust.

4. We begin from the beginning.

5. Hemanta walks in the flower garden.

adverb:

1. The girls sang beautifully.

2. Her phone rang loudly.

3. Hemanta walks slowly.

4. He leads wisely.

5. She draws superbly.

6. She swims beautifully.

Cognate object: Some cognate verbs are:- fight, dream, laugh, cry, live, sigh, smile, sleep, walk, talk, dance, die etc.

1. We fought a good fight.

2. She slept a sound sleep.

Extra:

  1. He slept a troubled sleep. (He slept, and his sleep was troubled.)
  2. He laughed a bitter laugh. (He laughed bitterly.)
  3. He dreamed a strange dream. (He dreamed, and his dream was strange.)
  4. He walked their walk and talked their talk.
  5. He smiled a charming smile.
  6. He danced a cheerful dance.
  7. He died a painful death.
  8. He sighed a deep sigh. (Sigh meaning: emit a long, deep audible breath expressing sadness, relief, tiredness, or similar.)
  9. He live a peacefull life.
